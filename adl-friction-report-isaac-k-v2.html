Structural Summary

You're running a hybrid work schedule — alternating between home weeks and office weeks with a 30–40 minute commute — while serving as a primary caregiver for a baby outside work hours. Your FFI scores are largely stable, which tells us your individual load tolerance is solid. But the friction isn't in the domains — it's in the transitions between them. Every shift from engineer to parent happens without a buffer, and night wake-ups are quietly compressing the recovery window that would otherwise protect your performance. The good news: you already know what works for you. Exercise first. Do hard things early. Automate what repeats. The goal now is to protect those instincts structurally so they hold even on the hard days.

→ Your morning energy window is your highest-value asset. Protecting it — and building a transition ritual that doesn't cost it — is the single highest-leverage change available to you right now.

Part 02 Acceptance — Why This Is Happening
Primary Structural Constraint

Your peak morning window is unprotected — and the baby doesn't negotiate timing.

You already know what works: exercise first, hard things early, automate what repeats. That's a sound strategy. The problem is that it was designed for a life with more schedule control than you currently have. Night wake-ups compress your recovery and erode the morning quality your whole system depends on. The work-to-parent switch happens reactively — whenever the baby needs it — without a buffer. Over time, unplanned transitions accumulate into a chronic low-grade drain. This isn't a motivation problem. It's a constraint problem. The solution isn't to work harder — it's to build defaults small enough that they survive the hard days: the days you woke up twice, have an office commute, and still need to produce.

Part 03 Adjust — What You Can Do
Start Here — Quick Wins This Week
✦ Protect the Morning Window
Exercise before anything else — even 20 min. This is non-negotiable on low-sleep days especially.
Block your first 90 min for your hardest engineering task — before Slack, before standup if possible
Write tomorrow's first task tonight — so you don't decide it in the morning
✦ Work-to-Parent Transition
Keep your existing break habit — it's working. Now formalize it: same cue, same length, every day
Add a physical cue to the break (change clothes, wash hands) to signal the mode switch to your nervous system
No reopening work apps or Slack for 30 min after caregiver mode begins
✦ Automate What Repeats
Set a recurring grocery order — remove one weekly decision entirely
Assign cleaning tasks to fixed days on a recurring calendar — stops the avoidance loop
Identify one more recurring task this week that can be automated or scheduled — add it to the system
✦ Office Week Defaults
Batch all meetings and collaboration on office days — protect home weeks for deep work
Pack your bag the night before — remove one morning decision on commute days
Pre-decide dinner for office days — commute eats time; don't also spend energy deciding food
Sensory Regulation Strategies
◎ Deep Work & Focus
Consistent audio anchor (brown noise or lofi) for every coding session — trains focus response over time
Headphones on = do not disturb — both at home desk and in office
Keep a physical notepad at desk for interruption capture — note it, return to task immediately
◎ Sleep & Night Wake Recovery
After a disrupted night, protect the first 30 min of morning — quiet start, no reactive inputs
On low-sleep days, explicitly reduce scope — one key task done well beats three done poorly
A 20-min nap around midday (if baby allows) restores working memory more than caffeine
◎ Physical Regulation
On days you can't do a full workout — 10-min walk still fires the cue and maintains the habit
Slow neck rolls + extended exhale breathing (4 in, 6 out) between engineering work and parent mode
Set a midday alarm — you tend to override hunger signals; don't wait until depletion to eat
◎ Interpersonal Recovery
After high-interaction office days, build in a 15-min quiet buffer before engaging at home
Communicate your low-bandwidth days to your partner — ask for reduced demands when you're depleted
Protect one block of solo time weekly — even 30 min — for cognitive reset without task pressure
Environmental Modifications
⊞ Home Work Environment
Create a physical boundary for "work mode" at home — even a dedicated chair or desk corner
Close work setup visually at end of day — visual closure reinforces the transition
Keep baby supplies outside the work zone — reduce context bleed between roles
⊞ Digital Environment
One task system only — pick it, commit to it, stop maintaining multiple lists
Silence non-critical Slack and email during your first deep work block — protect the morning window
Batch async replies into one or two windows per day — stop treating messages as interrupts
⊞ Office Week Structure
Treat commute time as protected — podcast, thinking, or silence; not reactive Slack
Pre-decide the office day schedule the night before — no morning decision overhead
Batch collaboration and meetings on office days — don't replicate them on home weeks
⊞ Household Structural Defaults
Cleaning: assign specific tasks to specific fixed days — no re-deciding, no avoidance spiral
Weekly family logistics check-in: same day, 15 min, covers the week ahead with your partner
Automate everything that repeats: groceries, bills, household supplies — decide once, run on autopilot
