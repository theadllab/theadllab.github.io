SCATTER

Tools to reduce sensory input — so your brain stops chasing every signal in the room.

"I sat down to work then noticed a dish, then an email… two hours gone."
Why these tools? Highly sensitive nervous systems pick up more environmental input and respond to it more strongly. Every stimulus competes for attention. The brain gets pulled toward whatever is most immediate, leaving the original task behind. These tools reduce the number of competing inputs. Selected by Ahra Roh, OTR/L, ATP — not sponsored.
