01 · Consulting

Compliance gets you to the door.
Clinical expertise gets you inside.

Most accessibility evaluations check boxes. I find what they miss. I evaluate how real users — with motor impairments, sensory differences, cognitive load variations — actually interact with your product. What they can grip. What fatigues them. What sensory friction makes them quit. What they'll abandon after week three.

Beyond physical accessibility, I work extensively with sensory processing differences in adults — an area most product teams overlook. Through ADL Lab, I translate clinical sensory science into design frameworks teams can actually use, integrating Dunn's Sensory Processing Framework with current research in interoception and cognitive load.

This is what 14 years of clinical OT looks like applied to product design. Engineers and UX researchers can't replicate it.

Underlying Philosophy

"Accessibility is not a magic wand to cater every request. It facilitates and decreases the gap, not freely hands the result. That distinction shapes everything I bring to product teams."

02 · About

Fourteen years at the meeting point of bodies, environments, and tools.

"Products designed well for disability and sensory-diverse users work better for everyone. That's not a theory — it's what universal design looks like in practice."

I'm an OT-Tech Specialist (Occupational Therapy + Technology) — bringing 14+ years of clinical OT into the rooms where technology is designed. I've spent those years watching real users navigate real products in real homes, classrooms, and rehab settings.

My work bridges three worlds that rarely meet: the clinical literature on how human bodies and nervous systems actually function, the product teams shipping the next generation of consumer and medical technology, and the daily reality of users who'll be living with what those teams build.
