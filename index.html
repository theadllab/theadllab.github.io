Consulting

Compliance gets you to the door.
Clinical expertise gets you inside.

Most accessibility reviews check boxes. I evaluate how real users — with SCI, motor impairments, sensory sensitivities, and cognitive load differences — actually interact with your product. What they can grip. What fatigues them. What they'll abandon.

This is what 14 years of clinical OT looks like applied to product design. Engineers and UX researchers can't replicate it. It comes from watching real patients navigate real products in real homes.

I work with teams building medical devices, wearables, rehabilitation technology, home appliances, and consumer products for disability and aging markets.

Product usability evaluation

Physical, sensory, and cognitive disability lens — SCI, motor impairments, grip and reach, fatigue, sensory load. I find what your testing misses.

Design recommendations

Actionable, prioritized guidance your team can implement — not compliance checklists. Specific changes, specific reasons.

User testing advisory

Protocol design for disability user testing — what to test, who to recruit, what to observe. Build the right study before you run it.

Team consultation

About

"Accessibility isn't a legal standard to meet. It's a design problem to solve."

I've spent 14 years as an occupational therapist watching patients — intelligent, capable people — struggle with products that were designed without them in mind. A handle that can't be gripped. A door that requires leverage a wheelchair user doesn't have. A device that looks accessible but isn't.

Products designed well for disability users work better for everyone. That's not a theory — it's what universal design looks like in practice. And it's what I bring to product teams who want to build it right.
