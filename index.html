01 · Why OT-Tech

A clinically trained specialist who reads what happens between a human and a tool — sitting next to UX, accessibility, and AT, not replacing them.

I bridge occupational science, the study of how humans actually perform tasks, with product design. That means 14 years of clinical practice plus real-world observation of how everyday people navigate tools, spaces, and technology in their daily lives. It's a specific combination that's still rare in product rooms.

Alongside UX Research

They study behavior.
I add the function layer.

UX researchers follow what users say and do. I add evaluation of the physical, sensory, and cognitive cost of doing it — fatigue, friction, and breakdown points that often sit underneath observed behavior.

Alongside Accessibility

They audit compliance.
I add performance.

Accessibility consultants check whether a product meets standards. I add a read on whether the product actually works for the human using it — across the full spectrum of how people function.

Alongside AT Specialists

They fit tools to users.
I read the system.

ATPs (myself included) fit specific devices to specific users. In a product context, I extend that lens to the full human-tool-environment relationship — across any product, any context.

Manifesto

Technology is any tool that helps a human perform a task — a spoon, a wearable, an AI agent. What's often missing from product rooms isn't more technology. It's the lens to know whether it actually works for the human using it.

02 · Collaboration

Open to a range of conversations, not a fixed service menu.

ADL Lab is in its foundation year. I'm building toward a consulting practice in OT-Tech, with first paid engagements opening up in late 2026 and into 2027. In the meantime, I'm open to a wide range of conversations rather than packaged services. If any of these resonate with what you or your team is working on, reach out.

  • Companies or product teams curious about an OT lens on what you're building — consumer products across digital, physical, and spatial categories.
  • Higher education programs (design, engineering, architecture, rehab engineering, OT, AT) looking for a guest speaker or workshop on OT frameworks applied to product design.
  • Researchers and practitioners exploring human function, AT, sensory processing, cognitive load, or consumer product design — open to collaboration or exchange.
  • Students in higher education working at the intersection of OT, rehab engineering, product design, digital design, or inclusive design — selective informal mentorship available.

I'm not packaging fixed consulting services yet. The shape of each collaboration depends on what's actually useful. Some will be paid, some won't, and I'll be honest about which is which when we talk.

03 · About

14 years at the intersection of bodies, environments, and tools.

"Most products are built around what technology can do. It's easy to focus on the end goal and overlook the actual experience of getting there. That gap is what ADL Lab is built around."

I'm Ahra Roh, an OT-Tech Specialist with 14 years of clinical occupational therapy experience across a variety of age groups and contexts. I founded ADL Lab to bring occupational science into the product design process, not as an afterthought, but as a structural foundation.

My lens covers the full spectrum of human performance: physical usability, sensory load, cognitive demand, and the spatial and environmental context people actually live and work in. That applies to any product: digital, physical, or spatial.

What I offer isn't a compliance review. It's a human-performance read on whether your product works for the human using it, and a strategic path to close that gap.
