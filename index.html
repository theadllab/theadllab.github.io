01 · Why OT-Tech

Not a UX researcher. Not an accessibility consultant. A clinically trained specialist who understands what happens between a human and a tool — at a level most product teams have never had access to.

You've been looking for this role — you just didn't know who to look for. I bridge occupational science, the study of how humans actually perform tasks, with product design. That means 14 years of clinical practice plus real-world observation of how everyday people navigate tools, spaces, and technology in their daily lives. That combination doesn't exist anywhere else.

vs. UX Researcher

They study behavior.
I study function.

UX researchers follow what users say and do. I evaluate the physical, sensory, and cognitive cost of doing it — fatigue, friction, breakdown points most user testing never surfaces.

vs. Accessibility Consultant

They audit compliance.
I audit performance.

Accessibility consultants check whether a product meets standards. I evaluate whether the product actually works for the human using it — across the full spectrum of how people function.

vs. AT Specialist / ATP

They fit tools to users.
I design the system.

ATPs certify that someone can use a device. I architect the full human-tool-environment relationship — any product, any context — built around how that person actually performs.

Technology is any tool that helps a human perform a task — a spoon, a wearable, an AI agent. What most product teams are missing isn't more technology. It's the lens to know whether it actually works for the human using it.

02 · Services

Where I work with product teams.

I work across digital, physical, and spatial products. The throughline is always the same: evaluating the relationship between a human, a task, and the tools they use to complete it.

01

Human-Performance Audits

A clinical read on how real users interact with your product — physical usability, sensory load, cognitive demand, sustained use. Not a compliance checklist. A functional analysis of what works and what breaks down.

02

Product Design Advisory

Ongoing input at any stage of product development. I translate occupational science into design decisions your team can act on, upstream — not as a patch after the fact.

03

AT Integration Architecture

For products that interact with tools, devices, or environments: I design the full human-tool-environment system, evaluating how components interact with each other and with the user.

04

Research & Testing Advisory

Protocol design for user research that actually surfaces functional friction — what to test, who to recruit, what to observe. I help teams ask better questions before they run the study.

03 · About

14 years at the intersection of bodies, environments, and tools.

"Most products are built around what technology can do. It's easy to focus on the end goal and overlook the actual experience of getting there. That gap is where I work."

I'm Ahra Roh, an OT-Tech Specialist with 14 years of clinical occupational therapy experience across a variety of age groups and contexts. I founded ADL Lab to bring occupational science into the product design process, not as an afterthought, but as a structural foundation.

My lens covers the full spectrum of human performance: physical usability, sensory load, cognitive demand, and the spatial and environmental context people actually live and work in. That applies to any product: digital, physical, or spatial.

What I offer isn't a compliance review. It's a human-performance read on whether your product works for the human using it, and a strategic path to close that gap.
